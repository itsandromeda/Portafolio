Long version (Skip)

My first job was when I was 19 when I decided to turn my hobby into a small business and I opened up an online handmade jewelry shop on Facebook having in mind that I was going to learn a lot about e-commerce to some extent. It was an amazing learning experience where I developed a strong interest on entrepreneurship, branding and the way my customers experienced the shopping process, (UX got a lot of my attention once I discovered it too later on). The project helped me develop my organization, planning, detail oriented and photography skills.

I've learnt many things by myself, one of them was this language, I started speaking english quite fluently since I was about 14. My interest on social issues came up right after that, since learning the language opened up a whole new world of information to my eyes and I had access to lots of new things. Before I got into Laboratoria I started another entrepreneurship adventure, but this time I wanted to focus on social issues that were close to my heart such as environmental pollution, sustainability and cruelty-free products.

I'm currently looking to become a part of a design-driven organization, I'd like to work closely with agile methodologies and adapt to new web development standards. One of my long term life goals is to create things that generate new oportunities for other people and things that solve real world problems, I've got a long way to go.

I believe that with a computer in my hands and an internet connection anything is possible, I can learn just about anything that I commit myself to; it's basically my motto and it definitely helped me along the way while attending Laboratoria where I learned a lot of things from scratch while humbly getting a few acknowledgements from the program.
